Long Information Graphic:

Flooding on the Red River of the North

This longform graphic depicts a bivariate analysis of the number of FEMA Federal Disaster Decarations by county against annual precipitation in Minnesota. The story told is focused on the Red River of the North River Valley along the Minnesota and North Dakota border. The counties along the River regularly have the lowest average annual precipitation, yet have the most Disaster Declarations of all Minnesota counties. The bivariate analysis highlighted this phenomenon in this corner of the state. Supporting analysis details geological and hydrologic reasons for this recurring problem, as well as some of the ways local officials have tried to mitigate the problem. Processing of the spatial data was accomplished with ArcGIS, and then design was completed with Adobe Illustrator.

Tour de Oz:

International Bike Tour Goes Down Under

Tour de Oz was a fictional bike race for while we were to design a route map. The exercise required the route to meet certain criteria in terms of length and composition. A primary component required the gathering and preparing elevation data to include in the map, and then to visualize it in a way that would be useful to a user. In my case, I used a digital elevation model. Preliminary processing was done with Spatial Analyst tools in ArcGIS and then finishing work on the hillshade affect was perfected in Adobe Photoshop. Design of the raster and vector map was accomplished with Adobe Illustrator.
